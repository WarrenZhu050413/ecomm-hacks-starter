Technique 1 of 10

Blur + Darken

Gaussian blur removes detail while darkening creates depth. Perfect for text-heavy UIs where background shouldn't compete for attention.

filter: blur(8px) brightness(0.4);
transform: scale(1.1); /* hide blur edges */
"In monochrome, we find focus."
Technique 2 of 10

Grayscale

Removes color entirely, creating a timeless, serious mood. Works well with accent colors in the UI that pop against the gray.

filter: grayscale(100%) brightness(0.5);
"Nostalgia is a warm filter on cold memories."
Technique 3 of 10

Sepia

Warm, nostalgic tones reminiscent of old photographs. Creates a sense of history and warmth. Pair with amber accents.

filter: sepia(80%) brightness(0.5) saturate(1.2);
"Two colors tell a thousand stories."
Technique 4 of 10

Duotone

Maps image to two colors, creating a bold, graphic look. Popular in modern design. Adjust hue-rotate for different color schemes.

filter: grayscale(100%) brightness(0.6)
sepia(100%) hue-rotate(180deg) saturate(2);
"Focus draws the eye to what matters."
Technique 5 of 10

Vignette

Darkens edges to draw attention to the center. Classic photography technique that creates depth and focus.

::after {
background: radial-gradient(ellipse at center,
transparent 20%, rgba(0,0,0,0.8) 100%);
}
"Brand colors unify the experience."
Technique 6 of 10

Color Overlay with Blend Mode

Applies brand colors using CSS blend modes. Multiply darkens while preserving luminosity. Great for brand consistency.

::after {
background: linear-gradient(135deg, #1e1b4b, #4c1d95);
mix-blend-mode: multiply;
}
"Contrast reveals what subtlety hides."
Technique 7 of 10

High Contrast

Amplifies the difference between light and dark areas. Creates drama and visual impact. Use sparingly.

filter: contrast(1.5) brightness(0.5) saturate(0.8);
"A whisper of color speaks volumes."
Technique 8 of 10

Desaturate + Tint

Reduces saturation then adds a subtle color cast. More nuanced than full grayscale, maintains some warmth.

img { filter: saturate(0.3) brightness(0.5); }
::after {
background: rgba(99, 102, 241, 0.3);
mix-blend-mode: color;
}
"Layers of darkness frame the light."
Technique 9 of 10

Gradient Overlay

Multi-stop gradient creates areas of varying darkness. Perfect for placing UI elements in darker zones while keeping image visible.

::after {
background: linear-gradient(180deg,
rgba(0,0,0,0.8) 0%, rgba(0,0,0,0.2) 40%,
rgba(0,0,0,0.2) 60%, rgba(0,0,0,0.9) 100%);
}
"Imperfection adds character."
Technique 10 of 10

Noise/Grain Texture

Adds film-like grain using SVG noise. Creates organic, tactile feel. Reduces the "digital" look of processed images.

::after {
background-image: url("data:image/svg+xml,...");
opacity: 0.15;
mix-blend-mode: overlay;
}
